I never knew my mom had a dog, one of those large-breed but lean black dogs.  
I asked her about the surprise, "Why haven’t I seen or heard of the dog?"

"I rarely see the dog myself," Mom replied.

"Are you close to him?" I asked.

"Not really. I’m too busy to take good care of him."

I leaned toward the dog while sitting down and hugged him, along with my mom. I had this strange thought that hugging my mom and the dog together would help them bond.

I stood up and headed toward the kitchen. The dog seemed happy to see me, even though it was my first time meeting him. I didn’t even know his name yet. He instantly grew close to me, following me around. When I looked down at him, I realized there was another dog with white fur, both of them sniffing me and circling around in a curious but friendly manner.

It really took me by surprise. I hadn’t realized there were two dogs in this unfamiliar household. I wasn’t even sure if my mom lived here. I hadn’t seen my dad anywhere.

The ground floor was extraordinarily large and empty for a residential place. “Do my parents live here?” I didn’t put much thought into it.

Both dogs ran to the large, empty "living room" They started playing, maybe too playfully; twisting together. It was getting a bit rough. I wanted to separate them before it escalated. But I didn’t know their names, so yelling wouldn’t work. I ordered them in a loud voice, but I didn’t succeed.

"Maybe it would be a good idea to take them out for a walk?" The thought dashed through my mind. I edged toward the "front" at one side of the empty living room. But it was too late to realize that wouldn’t be a good idea. I didn’t have leashes ready. Both dogs dashed out, passing me on both sides before I finished my thought.

They were running and leaping ferociously across a green grass patch between this place and the neighboring houses, mixing joy, aggressiveness, and excited barking.

"Beast energy…" I murmured.

I tried to come up with a way to get their attention and call them back. Remember? I didn’t know their names.

More and more dogs in the neighborhood started to join them. A small brown and white dog chased after them. A brunette (a neighbor?) with a ponytail was being pulled past me while I stood halfway inside and halfway outside the door. I felt embarrassed and wanted to apologize for causing this scene.

The afterglow of the setting sun still illuminated the neighborhood walls. I turned back inside. The room morphed slightly. The cold-colored ceiling lights were now on. The scene changed in a snap the outside grew dark. It was evening.

Suddenly, a party appeared. There were many people who looked like tech engineers. I was still near the entrance. More people were coming in. Quite a few seemed to know each other and were greeting a tall, overweight, nerdy-looking white guy with glasses. He was someone I had added on WeChat but had never spoken to or seen in real life. An American residing in Shenzhen, building his own VR goggles.

A shorter Asian guy with a ponytail and hooded eyes, wearing glasses and a thick winter jacket, stood right in front of me. He looked like someone I occasionally saw at the office but didn’t personally know. He was surveying the room, his body facing the center of a circle formed by several layers of people standing close together, shoulders rubbing. It was getting crowded.

There was no one in the center of the circle. What was this?

I turned my head. A short Asian girl I knew but couldn’t remember from where appeared beside me. In a soft tone, she mentioned it might be a birthday party. I couldn’t recall her facial details, only her voice.

People were congratulating one another, celebrating, but not too loudly, not too giddy for a crowd of this size.

Suddenly, someone announced from the background, "Let’s sing and dance!" People began forming a chain, like a train, each person’s hands resting on the shoulders of the one in front. A guy stood behind me; I hadn’t seen his face. My right hand rested lightly on the shoulder of the guy in a light bluish shirt.

The train began moving, unstable at first. A cheerful song started playing, and everyone was singing.

Then, out of nowhere, a light skinned guy with curly dark hair, wearing a slim fit black leather jacket, swirled in front of me. He was taller; I couldn’t see his face. Fine. I placed my hand on his shoulder. The human train gradually moved faster and more smoothly.

Then I felt it, a new person behind me. His hand gripped my right shoulder forcefully. The pain intensified. I couldn’t turn my head back. The train kept moving forward, and keeping up with the pace was already overwhelming.

Finally, the train stopped. The pain disappeared. People dispersed into different rooms to mingle.

I slowly walked toward a large ballroom on the right. In contrast to the light-colored, residential-style living room, the ballroom was decorated like an 18th-century interior, dark wooden walls, glass chandeliers hanging from a tall ceiling. A few groups lingered inside, but the space still felt rather empty.

Both the leather jacket guy and the guy who had gripped my shoulder entered the same ballroom as I did.

The leather jacket guy looked Southern European or Latino. The other one who had grasped my shoulder, was a young Asian man with a childlike face, still marked with pimples. He looked at both the leather jacket guy and me. No words.

All three of us sat down against the wall. Then the young Asian man turned his back to us, put his foot up on a chair, crossed his arms over his backpack, and looked as if he were about to nap.

That’s strange, I thought.

The guy in the leather jacket opened up, "Do you know much about Neuroscience?"

"Not really, but I always --" dragging my voice.

"You know, we’re looking more into human brains," he interrupted. "I have this project to operate directly on deep regions of the human brain using cutting-edge micro-electromechanical and chemical interfaces while the subject is conscious..." More jargon poured out

*This sounds new to me, but controversial for sure*, I thought, though I didn't say it aloud.

"Forget about AI; this is the next frontier," he continued.

I was talking to myself:* Hey, this is controversial. Shouldn't we talk about the ethical implications first?*

While I was preoccupied with my own thoughts, the young man extended his body as if waking from hibernation. "You’re working directly on human subjects?" he asked. "I’m interested."

Having been cut out of the conversation, I turned my attention to a dozen people not far to our right. They were forming a circle, rotating slowly. Each person had both hands raised like arrows pointing toward the ceiling. In the warm wall light, their figures flickered in and out, dragging shadows across the wooden floor. It felt like they were circling a bonfire.

In a sharp transition, the warm wall lights went out. The ceiling lights turned on. The ballroom was now covered in a cold, dim glow.

Most of the people in that circle had their backs toward me, except one tall Asian man with glasses. He stared at me coldly.

I felt threatened.

He began approaching slowly, snapping his index finger and thumb together as if holding a short blade without a handle. Was it a blade? I didn’t think so, but I wasn’t sure. His arm swung as though trying to slash me.

I turned halfway, attempting to run, but my foot caught on something. A chair leg? I didn’t know. I stumbled and fell to the ground beside the leather jacket guy and the young man. They were still sitting there.

Should I ask them for help?

I quickly abandoned the idea.

I began crawling toward the exit of the ballroom, back to the “living room.” It seemed far away, but the light there was stronger.

I turned onto my back, still edging toward the exit on all fours. The tall man kept swinging his arm. I could see the blade appearing and disappearing.

Is this an illusion? A trick? A dream?

It is a sacrifice.
